1 Things Mentioned in Burns Poems

  1. Burns's murdering paddle struck fear in her breast. Who is this wee, sleekit, cowering, timorous beastie, whose best-laid schemes came to nought?
  2. This creature was condemned as an ugly, creepit, blastit wonner; detested, shunn'd by saint and sinner. What is this parasitic insect, which was seen upon the hat of a hypocritical lady at church?
  3. The Earth shakes at the footfalls of those strengthened by this item, which doesn't make pigs vomit (unlike its French counterpart). What is this great chieftain of the pudding race, a dish made from leftover sheep bits?
  4. Sometimes clad in hoddin gray, when honest he's royalty despite his poverty. Who is this person, who is this person, for a' that?
  5. Unlike her colleague, she's not dead a mile aboon Dundee. She skelps on through dub and mire, despising wind, and rain, and fire. Who is this grey mare, ridden by Tam, and sharing a name with our hostess?

2 Poets

  1. Outranking all Americans as the most-sculpted poet in America, this serial monogamist is the reason we're gathered here tonight. Who is the Bard of Scotland?
  2. O! lovers of great poetry, / I must say that very probably / Ye feel within your breast / That this poet is not quite the best. / Who is this man that on a day / In eighteen-hundred-and-eighty wrote a poem about the Bridge of Tay?
  3. Born in Edinburgh, shaped in his literature by trips to the Pacific, and famous for both his novels (including one about split personalities) and poetry (especially a collection of children's verse), who is this pirate-loving author of Dr. Jekyll and Mr. Hyde, Kidnapped, and Treasure Island?
  4. Knighted for recovering the crown jewels of Scotland, inventor of the name Cedric, and a father of Romanticism, who is this prolific author of such novels as The Lady of the Lake, Rob Roy, and Ivanhoe?
  5. This Catholic saint and founder of the abbey on Iona spread Christianity in sixth-century Scotland, has a name that means "dove" in several languages, and wrote two abecedarian hymns. Who is he?

3 Food and Drink

  1. Often described with abstruse adjectives and exceeding Irn Bru in popular lore, this "water of life" is famous around the world. What is this drink distilled from barley?
  2. This redoubtable food moonlights as a monster in Dungeons & Dragons, is usually served for breakfast, and looks like rather like a diseased hockey puck. What is this colored pudding made from blood?
  3. Their American name served as inspiration for a fictional Mississippi raft rider, but despite their Scots name these berries are blue but not blueberries. What is this Scottish name for a huckleberry or bilberry?
  4. A more prosaic variant of this food is sold under the Walker's brand name; that food is plain shortbread. This decadent option adds caramel and chocolate ganache. What is this shortbread suitable for someone very wealthy?
  5. Traditionally popular as shepherds' fare, this porridge-based drink is eaten with butter and/or whisky. What is this oaty fluid, of which the song says "Give my love (this) and butter"?

4 Places

  1. This place, Scotia's darling seat! / All hail thy palaces and tow'rs / Where once, beneath a Monarch's feet / Sat Legislation's sov'reign pow'rs. What is this city known as Auld Reekie or the Athens of the North?
  2. Conquered by Vikings, famous for its tartans, it is the historic heart of Gaeldom. What is this area, which is more elevated than the Lowlands?
  3. Accidentally given to James III of Scotland as a wedding dowry, it is now known for fishing, renewable energy, and fiddling. What is this archipelago off the coast of Scotland, famous for its ponies?
  4. O' a' the waters that can hobble / A fishing yole or sa'mon coble / An' can reward the fisher's trouble / Or south or north / There's nane sae spacious an' sae noble / As what firth, which is spanned by a famous cantilever bridge from Edinburgh to Fife?
  5. Legendary home of trooser-less Donald, this island is the largest of the Hebrides and the historic seat of Clan Donald. What is this island, whose namesake "Boat Song" describes the flight thither of Bonnie Prince Charlie?
